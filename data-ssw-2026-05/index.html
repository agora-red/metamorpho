CAC formula — what's included

CAC = monthly Sales & Marketing spend / new paying customers
  • Fully-loaded = paid media (Meta) + marketing & sales payroll + agencies (Orange, Bomba) + martech + 33% of founder salary (rest → product / G&A)
  • Denominator = new paying customers (their first month billed) — not signups, not free trials
  • Blended only — channel-level CAC not reported: signup UTM attribution covers only ~46% of vendors (no robust organic attribution), so per-channel breakdowns are not reliable

LTV : CAC and payback by cohort

LTV per subscriber ÷ CAC ($41). Payback shown here is cohort-realized = month at which cumulative gross profit per original cohort subscriber crosses $41. It runs slower than the standard SaaS formula (CAC / ARPU·GM = 4.7 mo) because churned subscribers stop contributing — mature cohorts pay back empirically in ~6–7 months, with the gap reflecting early-month attrition.

NRR & GRR — monthly

Net vs Gross Revenue Retention, USD. Gap (NRR − GRR) = expansion.
NRRGRR100%
Reading the swings: measured month-over-month in USD on a few-hundred-vendor base — inherently volatile. Main drivers: (1) FX timing — retained ARS revenue is converted at each month's rate, so a month followed by an ARS devaluation reads lower. The Aug-25 trough (~83%) maps to the Aug→Sep ARS move (1,310 → 1,375), not a churn spike — cancellations were actually below trend that month. (2) Argentine winter seasonality (Jun–Aug service slowdown → more contraction). (3) Dunning batches & small-base noise. Trailing-12m smooths these to NRR 95.6% / GRR ~92%.
